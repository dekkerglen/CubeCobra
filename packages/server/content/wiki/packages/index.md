A package is a named bundle of 2 to 100 cards, curated by a Cube Cobra user and shared with
everyone. Packages capture the card groups cube designers reach for again and again: a dual land
cycle, an initiative package, the ten signpost uncommons for a set of archetypes. Instead of adding
those cards one at a time, you add the package.

Browse them at [cubecobra.com/packages](/packages), reachable from the **Explore** menu in the top
navigation. Signed-in users also get a **Your Packages** menu, with links to their own packages,
their liked packages, and the package builder.

## Browsing and searching

The packages page lists every submitted package, sorted by popularity or by date, in either
direction. Each entry shows the package's title, who submitted it and when, its vote count, and an
image grid of every card in it. Clicking a card opens that card's page; clicking the title opens
the package's own page, which has a shareable URL.

The search box up top understands a few kinds of query:

- Plain words match against package titles and the names of cards inside them. Searching
  `reanimator` finds packages with reanimator in the title, and packages whose card names carry
  the word.
- `card:Griselbrand` finds packages containing that card, in any printing. Use quotes for
  multi-word names, like `card:"Black Lotus"`, and combine up to ten `card:` terms to find
  packages containing all of them.
- `user:username` finds packages submitted by a specific user.

A question-mark icon next to the search box opens the full syntax reference.

## Voting and liked packages

Signed-in users can vote for a package, and votes drive the popularity sort. Voting doubles as
bookmarking: every package you've voted for appears on your liked packages page, and your liked
list is one of the two sources the cube editor's package picker draws from. If you expect to reuse
a package someday, vote for it now and it'll be waiting in the picker.

Each user's liked packages are public, at their profile's liked packages page.

## Adding a package to a cube

There are two paths:

- From the packages page (or a package's own page), click **Add To Cube**, then pick which of your
  cubes receives the cards and whether they land in the mainboard or maybeboard.
- From your cube's [list tab](/wiki/cube-page/list), open the edit panel and click **Add
  Package**. The picker offers your own packages and your liked packages; the chosen package's
  cards join your change list on the board you're editing, so you can review and prune before
  committing.

Cards added from a package arrive like any other addition: they get the cube's default status and
printing preference, and nothing is final until you save your changes.

## Creating a package

**Create A New Package** in the **Your Packages** menu opens the package builder (signed-in users
only). Give the package a title, then add cards one at a time with the autocomplete box, which accepts a
specific printing if you name one. A package needs at least 2 cards and at most 100. **Submit
Package** publishes it for everyone to find, under your name.

Choose a descriptive title; the words in it (along with the card names) are what searches match
against. Submitted packages are public and can't be edited afterward, so double-check the list
before submitting. If a package needs to come down, site moderators handle removal.
