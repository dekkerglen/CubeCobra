Account settings live at `/user/account`, under **Settings** in the user menu (the one labeled
with your username). The page is split into sections, selectable from a sidebar.

## Profile

The public parts of your identity, shown on your [profile](/wiki/profiles) and next to everything
you post:

- Your username. It must be 5 to 24 characters, letters and numbers only, and unique across the
  site. You can change it at any time.
- Your profile picture, chosen by naming any Magic card whose art you want. Lotus Cobra
  supporters can upload a custom image instead.
- Your about text, up to 2,500 characters of Markdown, displayed on your profile.

## Change Password and Update Email

Two small sections handle credentials. Changing your password asks for the old one and a new one
of 8 to 24 characters, twice. Changing your email asks for the new address and your password.

## Display Preferences

Site-wide settings for how Cube Cobra looks and behaves for you:

- **Theme** picks light mode, dark mode, or following your device's setting.
- **Default Printing** decides which printing you see when searching cards outside a cube: the
  Scryfall default, the most recent, the first, or the cheapest. Inside a cube, the cube's own
  setting applies.
- **Default grid tightness** controls the spacing between cards in image grids.
- **Hide featured cubes** removes the featured-cubes section from your dashboard.
- **Check 'Create Blog posts' for cube change by default** pre-ticks the blog checkbox whenever
  you save cube edits, for people who post every update.
- **Disable Animations** turns off interface animations.
- **Use text over icons where possible** swaps icon-only buttons for labeled ones.
- **Disable Follow Notifications** stops the alerts you'd get when someone follows you or likes
  one of your cubes.
- **Disable the cube board tray** hides the floating tray that lets you drag cards into your
  cubes from search pages.
- **Your cubes sort order** lists your cubes alphabetically or by last update, wherever they
  appear.

## My Images

Lotus Cobra supporters get image hosting. Upload JPG, PNG, WebP, or GIF files up to 10 MB each;
they're converted to WebP, resized to at most 1600 pixels on the long edge, and served from Cube
Cobra's CDN. Each image gets a copyable URL, a rename control, and a delete control, and the
section shows your usage against the limits of 1,000 images and 1 GB of storage. Hosted images can
serve as your avatar, as cube cover images, and anywhere else a URL works, like blog posts and
primers.

## Patreon Integration

Cube Cobra is funded through [Patreon](https://patreon.com/cubecobra), and this section links your
Patreon account so the site knows your tier: Cobra Hatchling, Coiling Oracle, or Lotus Cobra.

Two perks are managed here and in the sections above. Coiling Oracle supporters and up can place
one of their cubes in the rotating featured-cubes queue; this section shows your cube's current
position and lets you add, replace, or remove it. Lotus Cobra supporters get the image hosting and
custom avatar features described above. Unlinking your Patreon account is one button, right below.

## Notifications

The bell icon in the top navigation collects your notifications: someone drafted your cube, liked
it, followed you, replied to your comment. The menu shows recent ones with a clear-all link, and
an archive of older notifications sits behind it. What you get notified about is controlled from
the settings above and from each cube's own
[options](/wiki/cube-page/settings).

## Delete Account

The last section permanently deletes your account, along with your blog posts and drafts; your
comments stay but are anonymized. It requires your password, a confirmation, and one deliberate
speed bump: you must delete each of your cubes first, from their own
[settings](/wiki/cube-page/settings). There is no undo.
