Every cube on Cube Cobra has its own page, and that page is where almost everything happens:
browsing and editing the list, reading the primer and blog, running drafts, logging real-life
events, and digging into analytics. This section walks through each part of the page in detail.

## Finding your way around

On desktop, a sidebar on the left side of the page holds the main navigation. It has six sections,
each with its own sub-pages:

- [List](/wiki/cube-page/list) covers the card list itself, in five display modes, with sorting,
  filtering, and editing.
- [About](/wiki/cube-page/about) holds the primer, the cube's blog, and the full change history.
- [Playtest](/wiki/cube-page/playtest) runs drafts against bots, sample packs, sealed, and other
  formats, and keeps every deck ever drafted from the cube.
- [Records](/wiki/cube-page/records) stores reports from real-life draft nights, with decklists,
  match results, trophies, and win-rate analytics.
- [Analysis](/wiki/cube-page/analysis) charts the cube's curve, colors, prices, keywords, combos,
  and draft history.
- [Settings](/wiki/cube-page/settings) appears only for the cube's owner and collaborators. It
  covers cube metadata, options, collaborators, boards and views, custom sorts, draft formats, and
  restoring old versions.

The sidebar can be collapsed to a narrow strip of icons using the chevron at the top. While
collapsed, hovering an icon opens a small menu with that section's sub-pages. Cube Cobra remembers
whether you keep it open or closed.

On phones, the sidebar is replaced by a navigation bar fixed to the bottom of the screen, with a
second row of tabs under the header for switching between a section's sub-pages.

## The header

Across the top of every cube page sits the cube header: the cube's name, card count, like count,
owner, category badges, and a row of action buttons for sharing, exporting, comparing, and more.
The header and all of its tools get their own page: [Cube Header and Sharing
Tools](/wiki/cube-page/header-and-tools).

The header appears full-size on the list, primer, blog, and changelog pages and shrinks to a slim
bar elsewhere. You can also collapse it yourself with the chevron in its top-right corner; Cube
Cobra remembers your choice separately for each page.

## Who can do what

Anyone who can see a cube can browse the list, read the blog and changelog, run practice drafts,
view analytics, export the list, and leave comments. Editing is limited to the cube's owner and
anyone the owner has added as a collaborator. A handful of things stay owner-only: cube settings
like the name and visibility, blog posts, and deleting the cube. The pages in this section call out
those distinctions where they matter.
